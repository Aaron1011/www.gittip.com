Want to get involved?

Thanks for wanting to help! This is the place to start. :-)

Gittip is a new kind of organization called an “open company.” Anyone can volunteer to help with almost anything at any time, and everyone who volunteers gets to take some of the money that has been given on Gittip to Gittip itself—Gittip is funded on Gittip.

Where does work happen?

We work in public as much as possible. It's an essential part of our corporate culture.

1. IRC (Internet Relay Chat)
We hang out in the #gittip channel on the Freenode network, and this is the best place to become a part of the community of people building Gittip. If you don't know what IRC is but feel brave, dip your toes in here: http://chat.gittip.com/.
2. GitHub
GitHub is different than Gittip! GitHub is where we manage our source code and keep track of and discuss open issues. Diving in here is the second-best way to get involved.
3. Google Hangouts On Air
We do video conferences with Google Hangouts On Air, which means they get live-streamed and posted to YouTube.
4. Medium / Tinyletter
We have a “Building Gittip” collection on Medium where anyone can publish longer-form analysis and ideas. We also have an email newsletter called “Roll on, Gittip!” on Tinyletter, where we occassionally post summaries of work going on around the company. Here's the archive.
The primary audience for both of these is people building Gittip (unlike our blog, which is for the general public). If you want the inside scoop but don't need it in real time, subscribing on Tinyletter (here) or Medium (RSS) is a good option. For real-time, use IRC (above).
5. In Person
We're predominantly a distributed team, but we do sometimes come together at meetups and hackathons and conferences and whatnot. Catch as catch can.
6. Email / Text / Phone
We don't have private conversations if we can help it.
If you have a security-related matter to discuss, you can find Chad's PGP key on our security page.

What next?

Announce yourself on IRC and say you want to help, or if you're not sure about IRC or no-one is around when you show up there, then tweet “I want to help!” to @Gittip. Someone will respond and help you get started with your first project.

Woo-hoo! Thanks! :-)
